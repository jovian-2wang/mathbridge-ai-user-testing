Lesson 7 Summary
It is natural to think about groups when we have more than one group, but we can also have a
fraction of a group
.
To find the amount in a fraction of a group, we can multiply the fraction by the amount in the whole group. If a bag of rice weighs 5 kg,
3/4
of a bag would weigh (
3/4 · 5)
kg.
[Image description: A diagram showing kilograms and bags]
Sometimes we need to find what fraction of a group an amount is. Suppose a full bag of flour weighs 6 kg. A chef used 3 kg of flour. What fraction of a full bag was used? In other words, what fraction of 6 kg is 3 kg?
This question can be represented by a multiplication equation and a division equation, as well as by a diagram.
{?} · 6 = 3
3÷ 6 = {?}
[Image description: A tape diagram of 6 equal parts. Above the diagram, a brace from the beginning of the diagram to the end of the diagram is labeled "6 kilograms."Below the diagram, a brace from the beginning of the diagram to the end of the diagram is labeled 1 bag. A third brace that contains the first three parts is labeled "three ." Below the diagram, a fourth brace which also contains the first three parts is labeled "question mark bag."]
We can see from the diagram that 3 is
1/2
of 6, and we can check this answer by multiplying:
1/2 · 6 = 3
.
In
any
situation where we want to know what fraction one number is of another number, we can write a division equation to help us find the answer.
For example, “What fraction of 3 is
21/4
?” can be expressed as
{?} · 3 = 21/4
, which can also be written as
21/4÷ 3 = {?}
.
The answer to “What is
21/4 ÷ 3
?” is also the answer to the original question.
[Image description: A diagram showing cups and groups]
The diagram shows that 3 wholes contain 12 fourths, and
21/4
contains 9 fourths, so the answer to this question is
9/12
, which is equivalent to
3/4
.
We can use diagrams to help us solve other division problems that require finding a fraction of a group. For example, here is a diagram to help us answer the question: “What fraction of
9/4
is
3/2
?,” which can be written as
3/2 ÷ 9/4 = {?}
.
[Image description: A diagram showing the breakdown of 2.25 into groups]
We can see that the quotient is
6/9
, which is equivalent to
2/3
. To check this, let’s multiply.
2/3 · 9/4 = 18/12
, and
18/12
is, indeed, equal to
3/2
.
