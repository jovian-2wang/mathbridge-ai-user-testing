Grade 6 Whole Number Division: Equal Sharing and Measurement Division

Division can describe two different situations.

1. Equal sharing division:
The total amount and the number of groups are known.
The unknown is the amount in each group.

Example:
180 apples are divided equally into 4 boxes.
This means 180 ÷ 4.
The quotient tells how many apples are in one box.

A student can reason:
- 180 is the total number of apples.
- 4 is the number of equal groups.
- Since 4 = 2 × 2, divide by 2 and then divide by 2 again.
- Half of 180 is 90, and half of 90 is 45.

2. Measurement division:
The total amount and the size of each group are known.
The unknown is the number of groups.

Example:
How many groups of 4 apples can be made from 180 apples?
This means 180 ÷ 4.
The quotient tells how many groups can be made.

A student can reason:
- 180 is the total number of apples.
- 4 is the number of apples in each group.
- Ask: 4 × ? = 180.
- A nearby fact is 4 × 40 = 160.
- 180 - 160 = 20.
- 20 ÷ 4 = 5.
- So the total number of groups is 40 + 5.

Students should always interpret the quotient in context.
For equal sharing, the quotient is the amount in each group.
For measurement division, the quotient is the number of groups.
